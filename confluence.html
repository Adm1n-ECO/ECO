Where knowledge that belongs to no single network flows freely between all of them.

What this is

A commons.
Owned by no one.
Available to everyone.

Every network on EternalCurrent.Online holds its own stories, its own vocabulary, its own migrations. That knowledge belongs to the network. It is private, protected, and shared only within the relationships the network has chosen.

The Confluence is different. It is the layer beneath the networks — a shared vocabulary library built from the contributions of cultural scholars, community Keepers who choose to share, and verified subject matter experts who want their life's knowledge to outlast them.

No network owns the Confluence. No contribution can be taken back once it has served someone else's family.

A kinship term contributed by a linguistics professor in Toronto and validated by a community elder in Auckland and recognized by a diaspora family in London — these three people will never meet. But through the Confluence, their knowledge sits in the same place, available to any family whose story touches any of those threads.

This is the commons. It moves in all directions simultaneously. It belongs to the meeting itself.

How terms enter
Our methodology

How knowledge enters the Confluence — and what it means when it does.

01
Community Keeper contribution

Designated Keepers within private networks may choose to share vocabulary terms with the Confluence on a term-by-term basis. Each term is their explicit choice. Nothing is shared automatically. Attribution reads "Contributed by a community Keeper" — never the Keeper's name or network unless they request otherwise.

02
Cultural scholar review

Linguists, Ethnic Studies faculty, oral historians, and other subject matter experts review terms for accuracy, nuance, and what the definition may be missing. Scholars never access private network data. They work only at the commons layer. Attribution is tiered — anonymous to fully citable — entirely at the scholar's discretion.

03
Two-reviewer confirmation

A term is marked Scholar Verified only after two independent reviewers have confirmed it. A single expert confirmation is logged and visible, but does not achieve verified status. This prevents a single well-intentioned but regionally atypical reviewer from certifying something most speakers would not recognize.

A network's own Keeper is never subject to outside review. If a Keeper has validated a term within their network, that term belongs to the network. The Confluence holds what no network owns.
For scholars and subject matter experts

Your knowledge, held where it can keep doing something.

The Confluence is built for people who have spent careers studying what families carry across generations — and who want that knowledge to reach the living communities it was always meant to serve.

Contributing to the Confluence is not paid work. It is something rarer: the chance to have your expertise held in a place designed around permanence, connected to diaspora families who are actively looking for exactly what you know.

Your contribution is citable. It goes on a CV as community engagement. It is attributed the way you choose — from fully anonymous to fully named. And it can be removed at any time, for any reason, with 48 hours' notice.

No ongoing obligation. No advisory board. No platform relationship beyond the contribution itself.

How it works

"Would you be open to reviewing a small set of vocabulary terms?"

1
We send you a vocabulary review page — 15 terms, 10 minutes, no login required. You confirm, flag, or add nuance to each term in your area of expertise.
2
You choose your attribution level — anonymous, initials and field, or fully citable with name and institution.
3
Your review enters the confirmation queue. Two independent confirmations achieve Scholar Verified status. Your contribution is live in the library.
4
You can modify or remove your attribution at any time. Your name never appears without your explicit ongoing confirmation.

ECO Annual Diaspora Report

Aggregate findings.
Freely published. Freely citable.

Each year, EternalCurrent.Online publishes aggregate findings from across its networks — patterns in diaspora migration, vocabulary preservation rates across generations, cross-cultural kinship similarities. No individual, no network, no household is ever identified. The report is publicly available and freely citable in academic work.
